Instructions

  • Enter a class title and add the correct number of students
  • Then enter their names and estimate the number of repeats to learn a topic
  • Have students decide >= 2 other people they can teach the topic to, once they understand it

  • Save your class to local storage. The class name will get it back

  • Decide which students will probably pre-learn the material and
  • Click pre-learn and student-pre-teach if appropriate for the class.
  • Then click how many times you teach the material or click the button that also allows students to reteach to their connected students.
  • See how many teaching attempts are needed for the majority of the class to learn the material


  • This mimics some of the concepts of neural networks.

    • Background

    By Jeremy Ellis online @rocksetta High School Robotics and Machine Learning Teacher. BC, Canada.
    Since the early 1990's I have been coding neural networks, mainly unsuccessfully, but have improved in the last few years using both TensorflowJS and EdgeImpulse.com

    Many people have modelled machine learning as a simplification of how the brain works, but I have had an interest in Machine Learning to help me understand how we learn.

    This website is a way to look at how a classroom and the job of teaching might benefit from using a machine learning approach to learning! Have fun.
